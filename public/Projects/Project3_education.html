Course description

Go beyond the traditional canon of English literature to study texts from many different genres and contexts. On this course you will have opportunities to engage with classic texts by writers like Shakespeare, Dickens and Austen, but may also find yourself reading:

  • Native American writing
  • Radical poetry
  • Avant-garde writing
  • Speculative fiction
  • LGBTQ+ rights literature
  • Experimental writing
  • Literature of the Caribbean

As you progress through the degree you will become an increasingly skilled reader, writer and critic. A degree in English Literature will help you build personal and professional skills that can lead to many exciting careers. Throughout the course you will build your confidence in:

  • Analysing
  • Writing
  • Researching
  • Forming arguments
  • Working collaboratively
  • Communicating ideas

As part of our commitment to helping our students gain professional skills and experience, this course includes a dedicated employability module. This allows you to gain professional experience in a setting relevant to your career goals.

What I loved about the course

“I chose York St John’s English Literature course specifically for its wonderfully diverse reading lists and modules. My favourite modules included: Mapping America, Theorising Literature: Power and Identity, Gender and Sexualities and American Radicals: Outside the Canon. I wanted to delve beyond the canon into uncharted literary territory for me and discover new voices, perspectives and forms of literature. And that I did! I cannot even begin to list all the incredible authors whose work I had the pleasure of reading over my degree but it was a truly rounding experience and I believe I am all the better for it. At York St John, I read across time, places and lives that are different from my own and gained a deeper understanding of myself and the world around me.”
